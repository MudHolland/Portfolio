The three most important differences between Rousseau and Hobbes are:

  • View of Human Nature: Hobbes believed that humans are naturally selfish and competitive, while Rousseau believed that humans are naturally good but corrupted by society.
  • Social Contract: Hobbes believed that individuals must surrender some of their individual rights to a strong government in order to maintain social order, while Rousseau believed in a more democratic social contract in which individuals would have more say in the governance of society.
  • Role of the State: Hobbes believed in a strong centralized state with an absolute monarch, while Rousseau believed in a decentralized state with power held by the people themselves.
