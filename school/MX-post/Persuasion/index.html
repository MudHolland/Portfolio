Robert Cialdini's 6 principles of persuasion

  1. Scarcity: we want more of things there are less of
  2. Authority: we will follow credible knowledgeable experts
  3. Liking: we prefer to say yes to those we like (physical, likeness, flattery)
  4. Social proof: we look to the actions of others to determine our own
  5. Reciprocity: we feel obligated to give when we receive
  6. Consistency: we want to be consistent with things we have previously said or done

The last two principles are harder to apply, since they need more than one moment of contact.
