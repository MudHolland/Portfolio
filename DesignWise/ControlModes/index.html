Nintendo controllers

Because Nintendo controllers have a spurious and interesting revolutionary path.

Sony Playstation controllers

PlayStation controller because that controller had a clearer evolution path, whereas Nintendo designed controllers with revolutionary (game) control systems in mind.

Playstation 1: controller

Connectivity

  • Wired

Inputs

  • D-pad
  • 4 action buttons (cross, circle, square, triangle)
  • 2 utility buttons (select, start)
  • 4 shoulder buttons (L1, L2, R1, R2)

Outputs

  • None

Playstation 1: Dual Analog controller

Connectivity

  • Wired

Inputs

  • D-pad
  • 4 action buttons (cross, circle, square, triangle)
  • 2 utility buttons (select, start)
  • 1 switch (analog)
  • 4 shoulder buttons (L1, L2, R1, R2)
  • 2 Analog sticks
  • 2 clickable sticks (L3, R3)

Outputs

  • Analog button on/off indicator

Playstation 1: DualShock

Playstation 2: DualShock 2

  • Wired

Inputs

  • Pressure-sensitive D-pad
  • 4 pressure-sensitive action buttons (cross, circle, square, triangle)
  • 2 utility buttons (select, start)
  • 1 switch (analog on/off)
  • 4 pressure-sensitive shoulder buttons (L1, L2, R1, R2)
  • 2 analog sticks
  • 2 clickable sticks (L3, R3)

Outputs

  • Analog button on/off indicator
  • Force feedback

Playstation 3: SixAxis

  • Wired (miniUSB)
  • Wireless (Bluetooth)

Inputs

  • Pressure-sensitive D-pad
  • 4 pressure-sensitive action buttons (cross, circle, square, triangle)
  • 3 utility buttons (select, start, home)
  • 1 switch (analog on/off)
  • 2 pressure-sensitive shoulder buttons (L1, R1)
  • 2 analog shoulder buttons (L2, R2)
  • 2 analog sticks
  • 2 clickable sticks (L3, R3)
  • Accelerometer/gyroscope

Outputs

  • Player number (4 leds)
